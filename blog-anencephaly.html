Our Journey of Love: With an Anencephaly Baby.

✍️ Ariezz Augusto · 📅 February 7, 2025 · ⏱ 4 min read
← Back to all stories

May mga sandali sa buhay na muling humuhubog sa atin — mga sandali ng saya, lungkot, tapang, at di-masukat na pagmamahal. Ang aming paglalakbay kasama ang aming mahal na anak, na na-diagnose na may anencephaly, ay isa sa mga sandaling iyon.

Bagamat maikli lamang ang kanilang oras sa amin, ang kanilang naiwan ay mananatili magpakailanman.

Our romance with my fiancée started in January of last year. We decided to just live together and stay in Quezon City. And after a few months of being together, our love bore fruit. We happily shared with our family and friends the result of the positive pregnancy test.

The Diagnosis: A Heartbreaking Reality

I still remember the day we heard the words that changed our world. At our routine ultrasound, the doctor's face grew serious. Then came the explanation — our baby had anencephaly.

Anencephaly is a serious birth defect in which a baby is born without parts of the brain and skull. It is a type of neural tube defect (NTD) that occurs early in pregnancy when the neural tube, which forms the brain and spinal cord, fails to close properly — and survival beyond birth was unlikely or no chance at all.

Causes and Risk Factors:

  • Folic acid deficiency during early pregnancy increases the risk
  • Genetic and environmental factors may play a role
  • Exposure to harmful substances (certain medications, toxins, or infections) during pregnancy

Symptoms and Diagnosis:

  • Babies with anencephaly lack a major portion of the brain, particularly the forebrain and cerebrum
  • The skull and scalp are often partially or entirely missing
  • Ultrasound can detect anencephaly as early as the first trimester

Prevention:

  • Taking 400 mcg of folic acid daily before and during early pregnancy significantly reduces the risk
  • Prenatal screening and early ultrasound help in early detection
"How do you prepare to say goodbye before you even say hello?" — Paano mo paghahandaan ang pamamaalam bago mo pa man masabi ang "hello"?

Carrying Our Angel: A Time of Strength and Love

Despite the pain, we chose to carry and be excited to see our baby. We tried a different approach and consulted different doctors for a secondary opinion. Every kick, every heartbeat was a reminder that our little one was here, alive, and deeply loved. Some days were filled with sorrow, others with gratitude for the time we had.

The Birth and Death: A Hello and a Goodbye

The day of birth arrived with a mixture of emotions — anticipation, fear, and overwhelming love.

  • It was on the 31st of January, where my fiancée experienced a backpain and I needed to bring her to the birthing home.
  • She labored for almost 5 hours and luckily there were no complications with her delivery.
  • My baby didn't cry when she was born, so we needed to provide her with oxygen to help her breathe.
  • I saw my child struggling and fiercely fighting. I could see her suffering from the wounds on her head, desperately trying to get up as if she wanted to reach out to me.
  • Her fight for life lasted for more than 2 days. With every passing day, I could feel her cries, her hunger — like she was begging me to help her, but I couldn't do anything.
  • With every passing moment and with each of her breaths, I prayed to God to spare her from further suffering.
  • At home, you could see that she was really weakening, and her face was turning pale.
  • We even brought her to the hospital, hoping that she would live longer, but at 12:05 AM on February 3, she truly passed away. Goodbye, my child. You may Rest in Peace.

Grief and Healing: Finding Purpose in Pain

The days that followed were heavy with grief. The silence in our home was deafening, the empty cradle a painful reminder of what could have been. But in the midst of sorrow, we found strength in our baby's legacy.

We honored our child by spreading awareness about anencephaly, supporting other families facing the same diagnosis, and holding onto the love that would never fade.

Though our little angel is no longer physically with us, their presence is felt in the wind, the stars, and the quiet moments when love speaks louder than words.

Our journey with anencephaly was not just about loss — it was about unconditional love, strength, and the beauty of cherishing every single moment.

To every parent walking this path, know that you are not alone. Your child's life, no matter how short, matters. Their love, their impact, their story will live on — just like ours does.
