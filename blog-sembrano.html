Mt. Sembrano: A Hike for a Cause (sidetrip to Pililla Windmill Farm)

✍️ Ariezz Augusto · 📅 February 5, 2023 · ⏱ 4 min read
← Back to all stories

Mt. Sembrano is located in Pililla, Rizal. It offers a scenic view of Laguna de Bay, Talim Island, and an awesome view of the plains and surrounding municipalities. Mt. Sembrano has an elevation of 745 MASL or 2,444 feet. The mountain is situated between the boundaries of the towns of Jalajala, Pililla, and the town of Pakil in Laguna province — one of the nearest mountains outside Metro Manila.

My Trip to Mt. Sembrano

It's 2023 already and my last hiking experience was in January of the previous year at Mt. Balingkilat. I was longing for a new adventure. I had several plans — Sagada, Mt. Ulap, or Mapanuepe lake in Zambales — but the plans did not push through as one of my travel buddies, Mike, had a health issue.

When Ms. Marje, one of the event organizers, invited me to join a Hiking for a Cause in Rizal Province, I was hesitant at first since Mike and Mam Hides would not be able to join. But Ms. Marje convinced me because the proceeds of the hike would help one of the less privileged people — Nanay Odette.

We arrived at the jump-off point around 4 am and waited at the Barangay Hall for our guide. After a group prayer, we started the hike around 5:00 AM. The initial trail is on the road, passing by local houses that lead to a rocky path.

After one hour, we reached the Manggahan campsite and rested on bamboo benches while drinking fresh coconut juice. We continued on a 45-degree assault with bigger rocks, shrubs, and tall trees in a moderately steep trail.

Reaching the Summit

After more than 3 hours of hiking, we reached the North Peak — covered by cogon grasses. The view was amazing: Laguna de Bay below and the Sierra Madre mountain range in the distance.

We experienced 3 types of weather at the top — sunny, windy, and rainy. I also met Brownie, the adorable tour guide dog who knew the path very well!

Meeting the Gigantic Pililla Windmill

There is no entrance fee at Pililla Wind Farm. We arrived around 11 AM. The towering windmills are really huge and a marvelous sight to see.

We had a stopover lunch at Rambulls bakahan sa Tanay — the food was really delicious with unlimited soup of their famous bulalo.

My overall experience was great and fun — with some really great people you encounter during the adventure. Rizal province has so many exciting places to offer. If you're looking for a great adventure, just visit the place!

As what Jhong Hilario said: "The only impossible journey is the one that you never begin." You decide — One day or Day one.
