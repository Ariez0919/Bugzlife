DIY TRIP TO BAGUIO 2023

✍️ Ariezz Augusto · 📅 March 1, 2023 · ⏱ 3 min read
← Back to all stories

Baguio City is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the Philippines. Filipinos love visiting this place because of its cold weather, stunning views, affordable food, and rich culture and heritage.

Planning for a Trip

We already decided for a 3-day DIY trip to Baguio last Feb 25–27, 2023 before the short notice of Declared Holiday last Feb 24, 2023. (Kaya sayang at pede pa sanang gawin ng 4 days trip) Since it's a PanagBenga Festival in Baguio, it's expected that buses or transient houses were fully booked. Ma swerte kami at may nakuha pa kaming Bus papuntang Baguio at Sampaloc Bus Terminal. The Cubao and Pasay Victory Liner were already fully booked.

Going to Victory Liner Bus Terminal in Sampaloc:

  • From Araneta Center Cubao, ride the LRT 2 train and choose 'Legarda' as your drop off point.
  • Take a 7-minute walk to Victory Liner Sampaloc station.

Arriving at Baguio

Since we didn't have a booked transient house, we decided to explore the place and look for one once we arrived. We arrived at Baguio Victory bus terminal at around 3pm (at least 6 hours trip from Sampaloc Manila). Some bystanders offered us a transient house at ₱3,000 per night. Medyo mahal daw at punuan ang mga transient house because of the festival.

So naghahanap nalang kami ng mga Pension Inn or any budget Hotel. Luckily one taxi driver offered us some help. We found Pines City Pension Inn at ₱350 per head or ₱750 per room with 3 beds and hot and cold shower.

DIY Itinerary Tour

It's my 3rd time visiting Baguio. We decided to go to places we haven't been to yet — BenCab Museum, Mount Costa, Wright Park, and Tan Awan Village.

BenCab Museum

The BenCab Museum is named after the gallery's main artist, painter, and printmaker Benedicto Cabrera — a National Artist. The museum is divided into galleries showcasing amazing ancient tribal statues, figures, and weapons from the past.

Mount Costa

Mount Costa is the outdoor living room everybody dreams of. With its collection of 24 gardens inspired by the world's best gardens, it is one of the newest attractions near Baguio. It is located at Lamtang Pico Road, Barangay Puguis, La Trinidad.

PanagBenga Festival 2023

The Panagbenga festival is an annual flower festival celebrated every February in Baguio as an ode to the city's flowers. We witnessed a float parade of flowers and some local artists/politicians including Sen. Imee Marcos, Sen. Lito Lapid, Lovie Poe, and Benjie Paras.

Henna Tattoo at Wright Park

Me and Mike decided to have a henna tattoo at Wright Park. Thank you Henna Tattoo/Tiger Jade for featuring us on your Facebook page!

Last Day at Baguio

Before leaving, we enjoyed the activities and night life of the city — boating at Burnham Park, hanging out at SM Baguio (walang aircon pero malamig), and trying local food and delicacies.

Indeed, Baguio City's cold weather, breathtaking views, and endless list of scenic attractions are some of the reasons why I always come back to this place. And the best of all — the local people. Madaming magaganda sa Baguio.
